Responsive Website Design Resources

In this website, we barely scratched the surface of what can be done to make websites more responsive and accessible! There are a ton of things that we didn't cover, however if this subject has piqued your interest, I've compiled a list of resources for learning more about responsive and accessible website design. In the table below you will find the best resources I have come across regarding responsive website design!

Link Description
Kevin Powell's Conquering Responsive Layouts 21-day Challenge In this 21 day challenge, Kevin Powell gives you an overview of how to approach responsive website design in short, digestible, little bits. It purposefully takes its time building up small fundamental concepts, so that when more difficult ones are introduced you don't find yourself scratching your head. It is by no means meant to be a comprehensive resource, however it is a great place to start for absoulte beginners as you have to do quite a few challenges, and you get access to a great discord channel where you can ask questions to people taking the course at the same time as you. Also, it is completely free so that's great!
MDN's CSS Layout Cookbook MDN is one of the best free resources when it comes to learning anything related to website design, and responsive web design is no different. This is the most extensive documentation repository and learning resource for open web standards. It is maintained by Mozilla and Google employees and volunteers, and in it you can find a plethora of guides, exhaustive documentation of almost anything web related, up to date information on things current, deprecated, and experimental web technologies, as well as tons of examples and explanations geared towards beginners. By far and away one of the best resrouces for learnign about responsive web design and web development overall!
freeCodeCamp's Responsive Web Design Certificate Free Code Camp is a non-profit organization whose sole purpose is to make we development accessible to anyone. They have thousands of hours worth of content, and have helped thousands of people land developer jobs. They cover pretty much any area imaginable relating to web development and their tutorials and certificates are geared towards being as easy to understand and pain free as possible. It is a big time commitment to go through one of their certificates, as they take an estimated 300 hours to complete, however I have found their quality to be outstanding so it is well worth it if you have the time!
